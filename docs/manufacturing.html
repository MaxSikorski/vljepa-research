Why This Changes Everything

SALT eliminates the most complex part of vision AI training — making it practical for businesses that don't have a machine learning team.

Runs on Cheap Hardware

No EMA means 30% fewer FLOPs. The same model runs on a $35 Raspberry Pi, a Jetson Nano, or an Apple Mac Mini. No expensive GPU servers.

Zero Cloud Dependency

Every frame processed locally. Works in air-gapped facilities, classified environments, or simply where you refuse to send production data to someone else's server.

Deploy in Weeks, Not Months

Two-stage training decouples teacher from student. Mount a camera, collect images, fine-tune, and deploy to an edge device — with a clear 4-week roadmap.

How Manufacturers Are Using This

Four immediate applications — from print farms to CNC shops to robotics labs.

1

Visual Quality Inspection

Camera watches every print layer, CNC pass, or weld seam. The SALT encoder detects defects in real-time — catching warping, layer shifts, stringing, or dimensional errors before they waste hours of production time and material.

A 3D print farm owner catches a nozzle clog on layer 12 of a 400-layer print. Saves 6 hours of print time and $80 in material — per incident.
2

Robotic Vision

Self-hosted Vision Transformer running on-device. No cloud round-trip means real-time obstacle avoidance, object manipulation, and spatial awareness. Critical for autonomous robots operating in dynamic manufacturing environments.

A humanoid robot with embedded SALT vision navigates a warehouse floor, identifies parts by sight, and adapts to changing layouts — all processed locally in under 100ms.
3

Predictive Maintenance

Monitor equipment vibration patterns, tool wear, belt tension, and thermal signatures via camera. SALT embeddings cluster normal vs. anomalous operating states and flag issues before they cause failures or safety incidents.

Bearing wear detected 3 days before failure on a CNC spindle. Planned replacement during scheduled downtime saves $12,000 in emergency repair costs.
4

Inventory & Part Recognition

Camera scans a parts bin and identifies components by visual embedding — no barcode, no RFID, no manual entry. Works for custom-machined parts, 3D-printed components, and mixed inventory with no existing SKU system.

Receiving dock scans incoming parts against visual database. Auto-matches to open work orders and updates inventory in 2 seconds per part.

4-Week Implementation Roadmap

From camera mount to production deployment. Here's exactly how you do it.

Week 1

Capture Training Data

Mount cameras on your machines. Collect 500-2,000 images of normal operation and known defect types. No labeling needed for the initial self-supervised stage.

# Mount USB camera, capture frames every 30s
ffmpeg -f v4l2 -i /dev/video0 -vf fps=1/30 frames/img_%04d.jpg
Weeks 2-3

Train SALT on Your Data

Run the two-stage SALT pipeline. Stage 1 learns visual features from your images (no labels). Stage 2 distills that knowledge into an efficient student model. Fine-tune with a small labeled set for your specific defect classes.

# Stage 1: Self-supervised MAE teacher
python train_salt.py --stage 1 --data ./frames --epochs 50

# Stage 2: Student distillation
python train_salt.py --stage 2 --teacher ./teacher.pt --epochs 30
Week 4

Deploy to Edge

Export the student model to ONNX and deploy via ONNX Runtime on your edge hardware. Connect to your existing alerting system — Slack, email, PLC, or dashboard.

# Export to ONNX (runs anywhere)
python export_onnx.py --model student.pt --output model.onnx

# Run on Raspberry Pi / Jetson / Mac Mini
python edge_inference.py --model model.onnx --camera 0
Ongoing

Continuous Improvement

As your system catches new defect types, those frames automatically feed back into the training pipeline. Push model updates to your entire fleet of edge devices with a single command.

# Retrain with new data, push to fleet
python retrain.py --new-data ./flagged_frames
./deploy.sh --fleet all --model model_v2.onnx
